Our Curriculum Approach

Play-based. Purposeful. Built on Japanese philosophy. 
Inspired by Japanese philosophy. Designed for how children really grow.
We made a very conscious choice at WhizKids.
Instead of following a typical preschool system,
We chose to build our curriculum around Japanese philosophy, because it understands something very simple, yet very powerful:
Children don’t grow best when they’re rushed, corrected all the time, or made to perform.
 They grow when they feel safe, curious, understood, and free to try.
If you’ve ever watched your child completely lost in play, asking endless “why” questions, or trying to do something on their own, you’ve already seen this in action.
That natural curiosity… that confidence to try…
 That’s what most systems slowly take away.
We didn’t want that.
So we chose a way of learning that protects it.
A way that helps children:
Stay curious instead of becoming hesitant
express themselves without fear
build confidence through small, everyday wins
become independent, not dependent
Because let’s be honest, what truly matters isn’t how early a child starts writing or counting.
What matters is:
 Will they speak up?
 Will they try again after failing?
 Will they feel confident in the real world?
That’s exactly what this philosophy helps us build.
And that’s exactly why we chose it.
1. IKIGAI — Finding joy in learning
Think of Ikigai as what makes your child light up. That moment when they’re fully involved, curious, and happy doing something.
At WhizKids Jayamahal:
 We don’t push children into one path. We expose them to many.
How we do it:
Pottery, storytelling, sports, and role-play
Banking & selling activities (Little Shopkeepers)
No-screen bedtime storytelling initiative
Your child slowly discovers what they enjoy, what they’re good at, and where their confidence grows.
Skills built: Cognitive, Language, Emotional expression

2. OMOIYARI — Understanding others’ feelings
This is about raising kind, aware children who don’t just think about themselves.
At WhizKids Jayamahal:
 We actively teach empathy, not assume it will come on its own.
How we do it:
Good touch & bad touch awareness
Stranger safety activities
Playdates with parents (building emotional connection)
Group play and sharing routines
Children learn to express feelings and understand others.
Skills built: Emotional, Social, Language

3. KAIZEN — Small steps, big growth
Children don’t learn overnight. They improve through small, repeated efforts.
At WhizKids Jayamahal:
 We focus on progress, not perfection.
How we do it:
Repetition through phonics, numbers, and motor activities
Practice-based learning in daily routines
Gradual independence building
Every small win builds confidence.
Skills built: Motor skills, Cognitive development

4. SHIZEN — Learning from the real world
Children learn best when they experience things, not just hear about them.
At WhizKids:
 We take learning beyond the classroom.
How we do it:
Field trips to police stations, hospitals, parks, and supermarkets
Nature exploration and outdoor play
Junk food vs healthy food: real-life learning
They don’t just learn concepts — they see and live them.
Skills built: Physical, Cognitive, Environmental awareness

5. Hataraki — Learning by doing
Children understand responsibility when they actually do things themselves.
At WhizKids:
 We let children participate, not just observe.
How we do it:
Clean-up routines
Self-help habits
Role play like banking & selling
This builds independence early.
Skills built: Physical, Social, Responsibility, Motor skills

6. Wabi-Sabi — It’s okay to not be perfect
This teaches children that mistakes are part of learning.
At WhizKids:
 We create a space where children feel safe to try, fail, and try again.
How we do it:
Creative activities like pottery and art
Encouraging effort over results
Celebrating individuality
Children grow without fear of judgment.
Skills built: Emotional resilience, Confidence

7. MA — The power of pause
Children need space. Not constant instruction.
At WhizKids:
 We don’t rush childhood.
How we do it:
Balanced routines (play, rest, quiet time)
No-screen storytelling moments
Calm transitions between activities
This helps children reflect, absorb, and feel secure.
Skills built: Emotional balance, Attention, Cognitive processing

What This Really Means for Your Child
Your child isn’t just learning ABCs and 123s.
They are learning:
How to think
How to feel
How to express
How to interact
How to explore the world with confidence
And most importantly…
 They enjoy the process.
Because when a child enjoys learning, everything else follows.
